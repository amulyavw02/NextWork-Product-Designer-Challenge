Part 1 01

Task Completion
Redesign

I redesigned the task completion input from a plain text box into a lightweight documentation editor.

Learners can now add structure to their notes using text styles like subheading, paragraph, and simple formatting such as bold, italic, underline, strikethrough, and capitalization.

Why this matters

Task completion is not just a checkbox moment. It is where learners document what they did and turn their progress into proof of work.

The redesign helps learners make their notes more scannable, intentional, and portfolio-ready without leaving the task flow.

What changed

Before

Plain text with no hierarchy

After

Structured notes with lightweight formatting

What I'd ship next

I would test which formatting options learners actually use and simplify the toolbar if needed.

Part 2 02

Motion
Design

I treated motion as feedback, not decoration.

The goal was to make progress feel more alive without distracting from the learning flow.

Animation 1

Save Task feedback

A short button animation appears after a learner saves their task note. It gives immediate confirmation, adds a small branded moment, and helps the learner feel:

"Got it. Your work is saved. Keep going."

I kept it quick and contained because this is a repeated interaction.

Animation 2

Secret Mission reveal

I turned the Secret Mission unlock into a scratch-card interaction. The learner scratches the card using a NextWork coin, revealing the mission with a short confetti moment.

This makes the unlock feel more earned, playful, and memorable without pulling the learner out of the project.
